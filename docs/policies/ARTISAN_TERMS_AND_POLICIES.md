# Engel Fine Design — Artisan Terms & Policies

> **STATUS: DRAFT v0.1 (2026-07-23) — NOT YET LEGALLY REVIEWED.**
> This is the plain-language draft of the artisan agreement, assembled from the owner's
> decisions in `docs/manufacturing/PRODUCTION_RUNS.md` (each section notes its source).
> It must be reviewed by an attorney before any artisan signs it. Once finalized it becomes
> the in-app policy pages + the versioned agreement accepted at artisan onboarding.

---

## 1. The one rule everything follows from

**Nothing is fronted, and title passes at payment.** (§4, §4c)

Any work product EFD or another artisan produces for you — a CAD file, a casting, a cut stone,
a finished piece — **becomes yours when you pay for it, and not before**. Until the bill is
paid, it is not your property: CAD files don't unlock, castings don't ship, stones and pieces
don't hand off. When you create a work order, it is fulfilled, and you are liable for paying
that bill.

## 2. Your designs and intellectual property

- A design you create — or whose creation (e.g. CAD work) you have **paid for** — is yours.
  EFD does not take ownership of your designs. (§4c)
- A design whose creation bill you never paid was **never yours** (title never passed). EFD
  owns that work product outright and may produce and sell it. (§4c)
- **Collaborative designs** require a signed agreement between the collaborators. A production
  run on a collaborative design requires **the signature of every collaborator**, and the run
  records who funds it and the agreed payout split — the funder's risk is part of that
  negotiation. (§4e)
- All products are **released and sold through EFD** regardless of who owns the design.

## 3. Work orders — quotes, bills, and the platform fee

- **You see the price before work starts.** Every work order shows its cost up front (flat fees,
  or estimated hours × rate) and you accept that quote when you create it. The final bill is the
  actual cost, anchored to the estimate you accepted. (§4c)
- **You are billed when the work is completed** (passes quality control) — not when your piece
  sells. (§4.4)
- **Platform fee: EFD-fulfilled work orders carry a 20% markup on cost** (labor + materials,
  including vendor costs such as casting-house invoices) — the same markup EFD's wholesale
  repair work carries. Quotes always show the markup-inclusive number. (§4c)
- **Work you do yourself on your own pieces is never billed** — self-fulfilled work orders are
  bookkeeping (your earnings ledger), not invoices, and carry no fee. (§0, §4.4)
- Shipping and insurance are billed **at cost** — no markup on postage. (§4c)

## 4. Casting

- Castings ordered through EFD's casting vendor are **invoiced to you when the casting is
  received** (so the bill is the actual vendor cost, not an estimate) and **do not ship to you
  until that invoice is paid**. (§4.1)
- **Casting failures are the caster's liability** — you paid for ten castings, you receive ten;
  how many casting sessions that takes is the caster's business. This applies equally to vendor
  casting and in-house casting work orders. (§4.1)
- **48-hour inspection window:** after a casting is delivered to you, you have 48 hours to
  dispute any casting that fails your inspection. After 48 hours, delivery is accepted. (§4.1)
- **After you accept, it's yours:** damage during your subsequent work is your liability. You
  may scrap a piece (its limited-edition slot is released and its number retired) and order a
  replacement at your own cost. (§4.1)

## 5. Materials

- **Your piece, your materials bill.** Solder, gemstones, findings — whatever your piece
  consumes at the bench is yours to pay; EFD collects labor + materials on the completion
  invoice. (§4.3)
- Onsite EFD jewelers work from EFD stock (the materials charge is EFD's). Offsite jewelers
  purchase their own materials and are compensated for them through payroll. (§4.3)

## 6. Gemstones cut in-house

- When your design uses a stone from an EFD gem cutter, a gem-cutting work order is created
  (triggered by a customer purchase or by your production run). **EFD fronts the rough if
  needed; you pay EFD for the stone when the cut is completed**, and the stone is gated until
  paid. (§4d)
- The cutter is paid through the consignment rail, **net of any rough EFD fronted**. (§4d)
- A stone billed to you is billed at the cutter's listed gem price via consignment — the 20%
  work-order markup is never added on top of it. (§4c)

## 7. Quality control — two different gates

- **EFD standards QC** protects the EFD name: prong thickness, band gauge, setting sizes, and
  the rest of EFD's published design standards. Work EFD pays for is standards-checked by a
  peer before your invoice fires. (§3)
- **Acceptance is yours:** whether the work looks like what you ordered is judged by you, the
  paying artisan — via the 48-hour dispute window after delivery. (§3, §4.1)
- **Remote and solo work self-certifies:** a remote artisan working alone in their own shop
  (casting, bench) QCs their own work; your acceptance window is the check on it. Solo work on
  your own pieces self-certifies at the work order and receives its EFD standards review before
  it can be released for sale in the shop. (§3)

## 8. Shipping, custody, and insurance

- **Nothing ships until its invoice is paid.** (§4.2)
- You pay shipping as a line on your invoice, at cost. (§4.2)
- Every shipping leg carries **declared-value insurance**, billed through with the postage. (§4.2)
- **Custody:** while your piece is physically at another artisan's bench, that artisan is
  responsible for it (bailee liability). (§4.2)

## 9. If you don't pay

- **Immediate freeze:** an overdue bill freezes all of your run, work-order, and listing
  activity until it is paid. (§4c)
- **Liquidation after [X] days:** EFD may sell the unpaid work product. Remember §1 — an unpaid
  deliverable is EFD's property, because title never passed to you. What you receive from a
  liquidation sale depends on what of *yours* is embedded in it:
  - EFD's consignment cut applies first (EFD is selling), then the unpaid bills and direct
    costs are recovered from the proceeds.
  - You are refunded your **documented paid-in equity** — what you can prove you already paid
    for (your casting, your stones) — **and nothing more. All profit from a liquidation sale is
    EFD's**, retained as liquidated damages. EFD prices liquidation sales at its sole
    discretion. (§4c — equity-cap, decided)
  - If the liquidated item embeds a design you own (you paid for its creation), you retain your
    design rights and are paid the design fee/royalty on that one recovery sale. Continued
    production of your design would require a separate agreement. (§4c)
  - If nothing of yours is embedded (e.g. the unpaid bill *is* the design's creation), you
    receive nothing.
- Liquidation extinguishes the debt up to the amount recovered. Account-standing consequences
  persist. (§4c)
- **Two strikes:** a second default after a liquidation ends your participation on the
  platform. (§4c)

## 10. Sales, consignment, and EFD-produced runs

- Your products sell through EFD on **consignment**: you receive the gross sale minus EFD's
  commission. Production costs were already settled at completion (§3), so payouts are clean —
  no labor clawbacks. (§4.4)
- **EFD-produced runs of your design** (from EFD-owned drops): EFD funds the run and owns the
  product. You are guaranteed payment for any labor you performed and your associated design
  fees, plus **first refusal on the bench work**. A per-piece royalty is negotiated per run —
  default shape is *reversed consignment*: EFD keeps the gross and you receive the
  commission-side percentage. The terms are signed at run creation. (§4e)

## 11. Sales tax

- Provide your resale/sales-tax permit and your invoices are exempt, like EFD's wholesalers.
  Without a permit on file, applicable sales tax is added to your invoices per your state's
  rules. (§4f)

## 12. Acceptance

This agreement is versioned. You accept it at onboarding and re-accept when material changes
are published; your acceptance (version + timestamp) is recorded on your account.
